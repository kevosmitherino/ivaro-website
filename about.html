Built by a teacher who lived the problem.

Why we started Ivaro

Ivaro began with a problem one of us lived every single day. After sixteen years teaching, leading instruction, and mentoring other teachers, Ruby kept running into the same pattern: educators who love the work are quietly worn down by everything around it. Not the teaching itself, but the planning that spills into the weekend, the grading that swallows the evening, and the dozen disconnected tools that each promise to help and instead add one more login to juggle.

The technology existed to make this better. It just wasn't being built for teachers, or with the care their students' data deserves. So we decided to build it ourselves. Ruby brought the classroom: what teachers actually need, how a real lesson comes together, where the hours truly go. Kevin brought the engineering: how to build AI-powered systems that are secure, reliable, and trustworthy in industries where getting it wrong is not an option.

Ivaro is the result. One connected workspace for K–8 teachers, designed by someone who has stood at the front of the room, and engineered by someone who builds for the highest-stakes environments there are. Our goal is simple: give teachers their time, clarity, and confidence back, so they can focus on what drew them to this work in the first place, their students.

We built Ivaro because we believe teachers deserve intelligent tools that reduce overwhelm, support great instruction, and help educators focus on what matters most, their students.

What we believe

A few principles guide everything we build. Teachers stay in control: Ivaro drafts, suggests, and speeds things up, but every decision is yours. Privacy is a starting requirement, not an add-on, which is why Ivaro is designed with FERPA and COPPA compliance in mind and why student data is never sold or used for advertising. And the teacher matters as much as the lesson, so support for your wellbeing is built in alongside support for your instruction.

Ivaro is a Precision Learning Labs LLC product, built by educators and technologists who think K–8 teachers deserve tools made specifically for them, not generic software adapted after the fact.
